Disney Dining: Chef Mickey's

Located by the Contemporary Resort, Chef Mickey’s has a wonderful character dinner buffet. This buffet features your favorite disney pals Mickey, Minnie, Goofy, Pluto and Donald. At Chef Mickey’s the characters will each stop by your table for a few minutes for pictures and autographs. Make sure you have your camera ready as each character may only come by once during your dining experience. While you are awaiting the arrival of your next character, the buffet options are limitless ranging from appetizers like Roasted Vegetables with Pasta or Tomato and Mozzarella to entrees such as Spiced-rubbed Sirloin of Beef and Oven-roasted Lemon Pepper Chicken.
I’ll let you in on a little secret too, Chef Mickey’s is one of few restaurants where you can order “Cinderella’s Slipper” desert. This buffet is great for a once and a while treat, or if you have never been to Chef Mickey’s. Recommended for those who love the characters and value special moments with the characters that you won’t get at the park.

Reservations

Reservations for Chef Mickey’s are a must do, with reservations the wait time is significantly lower. On our last trip, the wait time was only fifteen minutes! While waiting to be seated you are able to take pictures at the PhotoPass location to help pass the time.

Special Moments

Like I had said previously, if you've dreamed of having the “Cinderella Slipper” and don’t plan on eating at Cinderella’s Royal Table, Chef Mickey’s is one of the few locations were you can order this specialty desert, as long as you call Disney’s Cake hotline and order at least 24 hours before your dining experience. Other cakes are also available within 48 hours notice. This cost is separate from the buffet. The “Cinderella Slipper” costs about $15.

Getting There

Getting to the Contemporary Resort can be a lot easier than some restaurants. If you’re staying on a Disney resort, you have two options of Disney transportation: monorail or bus. If you have the luxury of being at a resort on the monorail, then quickly hop on, and it will take you right inside the Contemporary Resort. If you choose to take a bus, remember the buses don’t go from resort to resort. Therefore, see what Theme Park is open late so you can get to and from the Contemporary by getting a bus to one of the Theme Parks and switching to the Contemporary bus, you can also go to Downtown Disney to switch buses. If you happen to be at Magic Kingdom, or take a bus to Magic Kingdom, take about a ten minute walk and you will arrive at the Contemporary Resort.

Characters

When you get to see the characters, you do not feel rushed and they spend a good amount of time with your party before they move on to the next table. Over the course of one hour, you will usually get to see each character once, depending on how busy the restaurant is. This one on one time with the characters is priceless and much more personal and enjoyable than the quick photo that you get at the parks. The characters also like to dance, tell jokes and put on a show. Watch out for Goofy’s mustard!
